A brilliant and methodical DETECTIVE

Sherlock Holmes is one of the most iconic detectives in literary history. He is renowned for his exceptional intellect, remarkable observation skills, and his ability to solve complex mysteries using pure deduction, often without relying on physical evidence.

Additional Information

Dr. Watson, a physician and army veteran, is not only Holmes' long-time friend but also his chronicler. He assists Holmes in his investigations and often serves as the narrator of their adventures. His iconic London address : 221B Baker Street in London is Sherlock Holmes' home, where he solves cases while sharing the space with his trusted companion, Dr. Watson. It is also a central location in many of the stories written by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle.

the Author: Sir Arthur Conan Doyle

Sir Arthur Conan Doyle (1859–1930) was a British writer and physician who created the iconic detective Sherlock Holmes. Inspired by his mentor, Dr. Joseph Bell, Conan Doyle introduced Holmes in A Study in Scarlet (1887), blending logic and observation into groundbreaking detective stories. Though he tried to kill off Holmes, public demand brought the character back, securing Doyle’s legacy as a literary legend. Beyond writing, he was involved in spiritualism, politics, and volunteer work, showcasing his diverse interests and impact.
